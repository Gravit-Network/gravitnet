markdown # Gravit Core Canon ## Gravit Canon Proposal Process Version: 0.1 Status: Active Applies to: Gravit Core Canon repositories --- # 1. Purpose The Gravit Canon Proposal (GCP) process defines the formal mechanism by which changes, extensions and analyses related to the Gravit Trust Protocol are proposed, reviewed and adopted. The GCP system serves three primary purposes: 1. Provide a transparent mechanism for protocol evolution. 2. Maintain a historical archive of technical and conceptual decisions. 3. Enable structured contributions from researchers, engineers, institutions and independent thinkers. GCPs are the only accepted mechanism for modifying the canonical protocol specification. --- # 2. Proposal Types Each proposal must declare its type. ## 2.1 Standards Track Proposals that modify the protocol itself. This includes: - ontology definitions - epistemology rules - threat models - governance mechanisms - mathematical core - protocol interfaces - default parameters Standards Track proposals affect system behavior. --- ## 2.2 Process Proposals that modify the governance or operational procedures of the Gravit ecosystem. Examples: - proposal lifecycle rules - contributor roles - governance mechanisms - review policies --- ## 2.3 Informational Proposals that provide analysis, research or conceptual discussion. Examples: - trust theory - epistemic analysis - threat landscape reviews - simulation reports - philosophical foundations Informational proposals do not modify protocol behavior. --- # 3. Proposal Scope Declaration Each GCP must declare the primary layer it affects. Allowed scope identifiers: ontology epistemology threat-model governance math-core protocol parameters Multiple scopes may be listed if necessary. --- # 4. Proposal Lifecycle Every proposal follows a defined lifecycle. ## Draft Initial proposal submitted by the author. The proposal is incomplete and subject to structural feedback. --- ## Review Proposal enters active community review. Editors verify formatting and completeness. Reviewers provide technical feedback. --- ## Last Call The proposal is considered technically complete. A final review period is opened to identify unresolved concerns. Default Last Call duration: 14 days --- ## Accepted Editors confirm that the proposal meets protocol standards and review criteria. Accepted proposals are approved for inclusion in the Canon. --- ## Final The proposal becomes a permanent canonical record. No normative changes may be made after Final status. Editorial corrections may still occur. --- ## Closed Proposal is closed without adoption. Closure reasons may include: rejected withdrawn superseded obsolete Closure reason must be documented. --- # 5. Roles ## Editors Editors maintain the GCP repository. Responsibilities: - assign proposal numbers - verify formatting compliance - manage lifecycle transitions - ensure archival integrity Editors do not unilaterally approve protocol changes. --- ## Shepherd A Shepherd is assigned to guide the proposal through the review process. Responsibilities: - coordinate feedback - ensure completeness - track open issues - assist authors in revisions --- ## Sponsor Standards Track proposals require a sponsor. Sponsors must be recognized contributors or maintainers. The sponsor confirms that the proposal is technically meaningful and worthy of review. --- ## Reviewers Reviewers evaluate proposals and provide feedback. Reviewers may include: - protocol engineers - distributed systems researchers - security experts - governance specialists - independent analysts --- # 6. Evidence Requirements Standards Track proposals must include: Security considerations Backward compatibility analysis Threat model impact Simulation or analytical evidence when applicable Proposals affecting the mathematical core must include supporting simulations or formal analysis. --- # 7. Activation (Optional) Some protocol changes may require network activation. If applicable, proposals must include an Activation section describing: - activation criteria - support thresholds - activation time window - rollback conditions Activation mechanisms may vary depending on implementation environment. --- # 8. Proposal Numbering Each proposal receives a unique identifier: GCP-0001 GCP-0002 GCP-0003 Numbers are assigned sequentially by editors. Proposal numbers are never reused. --- # 9. Repository Structure Proposals are stored in: /gcp Each proposal is a separate file: gcp-0001-title.md gcp-0002-title.md --- # 10. Historical Archive The GCP repository serves as the permanent historical record of the Gravit protocol evolution. All proposals remain archived regardless of status. Transparency of decision history is considered a core principle of the project. --- # 11. Canon Rule Changes to the Gravit Core Canon specification must originate from a GCP. Direct edits to canonical protocol documents without a proposal are not permitted.
